3.2 User Risk Assessment
3.2.1 Description and Priority
This feature allows users to assess their HIV risk level based on exposure and other relevant risk factors. Before taking the assessment, users will receive brief awareness information. After completing the assessment, the system will display the result along with feedback (e.g., 
High Risk
, 
Low Risk with Caution
) and recommended action steps.
Users will have the option to navigate to nearby 
health facilities
 through a “Get Service” button or connect with 
peer mentors
 for 
counseling
 support through a “Get Mentors” button.
3.2.2 Functional Requirements
REQ-002-1:
 The system SHALL allow the user to enter all 
the 
requested information required for the risk assessment.
REQ-002-2:
 The system SHALL 
analyze
 the entered information using the defined risk assessment 
rule
.
REQ-002-3:
 The system SHALL display the assessment result along with feedback and recommended actions based on the risk level.
REQ-002-4:
 The system SHALL provide a “Get Service” button for users to navigate to nearby healthcare facilities.
REQ-002-5:
 The system SHALL allow users to navigate to peer mentors for 
counselling
 support.
REQ-002-6:
 The system SHALL only categorize results as 
High Risk
 or 
Low Risk with Caution
, with appropriate guidance for each.
3.3 Guest Page Information
3.3.1 Description and Priority
This feature allows guest users to access a limited set of the application’s content without creating an account. Guests can view general health information and FAQs
,
 but cannot access full content, interactive features, or peer mentor navigation. The page encourages registration for full access.
3.3.2 Functional Requirements
REQ-003-1:
 The system SHALL provide general health information accessible to guest users without an account.
REQ-003-2:
 The system SHALL restrict guest access to basic content.
REQ-003-3:
 The system SHALL display FAQs 
above
 the footer on the guest page.
3.4 Health Information Access (Learning Module)
3.4.1 Description and Priority
This feature delivers tailored health content for users aged 15–24. It provides 
health 
information
 on
 HIV, sexually transmitted 
infections 
(ST
I
s), hepatitis, sexual and reproductive health, gender-based violence (GBV), and substance use.
3.4.2 Functional Requirements
REQ-004-1:
 The system SHALL display health information organized into six modules: HIV, ST
I
s, hepatitis, sexual and reproductive health, GBV, and substance use.
REQ-004-2:
 The system SHALL ensure graphical representation is consistent across all modules.
REQ-004-3:
 Upon login, the system SHALL display four main cards for navigation:
Risk Assessment
Learning Module
Get Health Service
Get Peer Mentor 
3.5 Healthcare Provider and Peer Mentor Information
3.5.1 Description and Priority
This feature helps users locate nearby healthcare providers and services, making it easier to access relevant care options. It supports navigation to facilities and peer mentors following risk assessment results.
3.5.2 Functional Requirements
REQ-005-1:
The system SHALL display provider details, including name, service, contact information, and address.
REQ-005-
2
:
The system SHALL provide users with the nearby health facilities based on the user’s location.
REQ-005-
3
:
 Following risk assessment, the system SHALL provide options to connect with a 
counsellor
 or peer mentor:
Option 1:
“Get Peer Mentor” → show list of mentors → provide direct contact (Name & phone only).
Option 2:
“Get Service” → allow location access → recommend nearby 
facilities (
health centre)

3.6.1 Description and Priority
This feature ensures users
 receive a welcome message after login and a reminder alert 
based on 
their 
last 
login
. 
3.6.2 Functional Requirements

 The system SHALL send notifications for

REQ-006-2:
 The system SHALL allow users to 
accept/reject 
notification preferences, including the option to turn off reminders or updates.
